Your Journey in the Cloud

A simple guide to help you navigate your cloud journey.

Matthew Mattoon
Published on July 12, 2019
Share:

Over the last several years of being a cloud consultant, I’ve noticed three stages customers fall into.

Phase 1: Lift and Shift

The key characteristic in the lift and shift phase is that we want to keep the systems that are migrated as close to their original state as possible. This allows us to focus on a successful migration without having to worry about introducing unnecessary complexity.

If you're in the phase, Entasis can support you by providing an Architecture Workshop where we assess the current state of your architecture, meet your key technology leaders, document, and visualize your current and the future state architecture. If you already have a plan, we can move forward and begin your cloud journey by implementing the data migration. As we do the migration, we will make sure you're following AWS best practices as you begin your cloud journey.

Phase 2: Cloud Optimize

The second phase is the “Cloud Opimization” phase. The key characteristic of this phase is that we are starting to take your existing applications and platforms and modify them to take advantage of specific features of the cloud. An example of this would be to move to a managed service for databases rather than having to manage it ourselves. This frees us up to focus on more important things like features and ensuring the application stack as a whole is healthy.

This phase is also the longest phase and is the highest value – this is because since we are dealing with established applications these all have different requirements and established user bases which we cannot interrupt in most cases.

If you find yourself in this phase, Entasis can provide assistance in a variety of ways. A common use case is helping teams move to AWS managed services. An example of this is moving from Docker or Kubernetes to a manged services like AWS Elastic Container Service and AWS Elastic Kubernetes Service. Our team has also enabled customers to take advantage of blue/green deployments and perform cloud native development using AWS Lambda.

Phase 3: Cloud Native

The final phase is the “Cloud Native” phase. The key characteristic of this phase is that your team is intimately familiar with high value cloud services and are designing applications from the ground up to leverage these services. This is often done in a microservices architecture, and it leverages all of the learning that your team has been able to do in going through the “Cloud Optimization” phase. Not many companies are in the Cloud Native phase because it requires a green field AND the experience of the Cloud Optimization phase to get that practical experience of optimizing your stacks in the cloud.

In this phase, Entasis can help by implementing microservice architecure development via AWS Cloudformation, Opsworks, and 3rd party vendors.

The journey to the cloud is an extremely rewarding journey for those who remain patient and seek outside help to augment existing teams. At Entasis, we enjoy complimenting your existing team so you focus on doing the activities that makes your business unique. Thank you for reading our guide, and I hope I get the privledge to shake your hand and say hello.
